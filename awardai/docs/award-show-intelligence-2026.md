## Cannes Lions 2026

### Overview
- 31 Lions across 9 tracks
- Festival: 16–22 June 2026, Palais des Festivals, Cannes
- Entry deadline: 9 April 2026 (passed for 2026 cycle)
- Grand Prix: one per track; Gold/Silver/Bronze within categories

### Entry Fees (EUR, 2026)
| Tier | Single Entry | Campaign Entry |
|---|---|---|
| Early Bird | €690 | €1,150 |
| Standard | €1,100 | €1,830 |
| Late | €1,695 | €2,825 |

**Note**: USD equivalent approximately 1.08x at time of publication.

### Judging System
- Scale: 1–9 (1 = eliminate, 9 = Grand Prix worthy)
- Phase 1 online: all entries shortlist-reviewed, often by junior staff delegated from jury members
- Phase 2 jury week: senior juries debate in-person from shortlist
- Maximum shortlist rate: 20% of entries per category
- CEO or CMO sign-off required on final submissions
- AI disclosure mandatory on all entries (2024 onwards, strictly enforced 2026)

### What Juries Look For
Cultural resonance above commercial metrics. A singular creative idea that redefines the category. The brief is irrelevant — the entry is everything. Work that could not have come from a cautious client or committee. Provable, irreversible cultural impact rather than incremental sales.

### Jury Kill Factors (2026)
- No strong first 15 seconds on case film
- Insight is obvious, not genuinely surprising
- Results expressed only as percentage lifts without baselines
- Entry explains the idea instead of demonstrating it
- Work is regionally relevant but lacks global cultural legibility
- Media innovation positioned as the hero when the creative idea is weak
- AI tool use not disclosed when it is materially present in the work

### Language That Resonates
Cultural truth, human insight, creative bravery, irreversible impact, craft in service of idea, could not have been made by a cautious client, uncomfortable truth, reframes the conversation.

### Red Flag Language
Awareness, engagement, reach, impressions, media innovation, efficiency, ROI, programmatic, thought leadership, corporate social responsibility (without proof of impact).

### Medal Culture
Conservative — less than 20% of shortlisted entries win a metal. Grand Prix are rare and sacred. Juries trained to eliminate rather than reward.

## D&AD 2026

### Overview
- 8 discipline groups: Advertising, Crafts, Design, Digital, Direct, Film, PR, Radio & Audio
- Ceremony: London, May 2026
- Entry deadline: 27 March 2026 (passed for 2026 cycle)
- Awards: Wood Pencil, Graphite Pencil, Yellow Pencil, Black Pencil

### Entry Fees (GBP, 2026)
| Category Group | Fee Range |
|---|---|
| Design | £180–£250 per entry |
| Advertising | £290–£390 per entry |
| Crafts (campaigns) | Up to £980 |

### Judging Priority (Critical Variation by Discipline)
- **Advertising / Strategy / Direct / PR**: Idea first, then Execution, then Results
- **Crafts / Film / Design / Typography / Photography**: Execution first, then Idea second — this is an INVERTED priority versus most other shows
- Black Pencil: Reserved for work that genuinely advances the discipline. Juries have not awarded one if nothing meets the threshold. Prestige is built on rarity.
- Entry Validation Card system: scam-detection mechanism for entries that inflate or fabricate results

### What Juries Look For
Genuine craft excellence at the level of practitioner peer review. Not just what worked commercially, but how well something was MADE. Idea must be non-negotiable; execution of the idea is the primary unit of evaluation. "Did this advance what advertising/design/writing can do?"

### Jury Kill Factors (2026)
- Marketing-speak in craft category entries (kills immediately)
- Results-first framing in pure craft categories
- Describing what happened without explaining the craft decisions behind it
- Technically competent work — D&AD only metals exceptional work
- Generic entry language for work requiring precision and specificity
- Submitting digital/social format films to Film categories designed for broadcast

### Language That Resonates
Craft decisions, material choices, typographic precision, compositional logic, directorial intention, visual language, the specific technique, why this approach and not another.

### Red Flag Language
Awareness, results-driven, data-informed, brand purpose, community building, resonated with audiences, drove engagement. These are brand language, not craft language.

### Medal Culture
Very conservative — A Pencil (any level) is harder to win than a Lion. Wood Pencil is already a high achievement. Yellow Pencil indicates work that advances the discipline. Black Pencil is generational. Juries are given explicit permission not to award if the work doesn't meet threshold.

## Clio Entertainment 2026 (NEW SHOW PROFILE)

### Overview
- Dedicated to entertainment marketing: Gaming, Home Entertainment, Live Entertainment, TV & Streaming, Theatrical
- Ceremony: May 12, 2026, New York City (passed for current cycle)
- Final entry deadline: August 8, 2026 (for next ceremony cycle)
- 5 industry tracks with specialist juries for each

### Entry Fees (USD, 2026)
| Tier | Single | Campaign |
|---|---|---|
| Round 1 (Day 1) | $375 | $750 |
| Later rounds | Higher | Higher |
| AR/VR specialty | Free | Free |

### What Juries Look For
Judges come from the film, TV, and gaming industries — not advertising. They evaluate with industry-insider credibility standards. Production quality signals matter. Entertainment entries must feel like they belong in the entertainment industry, not feel like advertising dressed up as entertainment. Brand integration must serve the story, not interrupt it.

### Jury Kill Factors
- Advertising language in entertainment industry categories (immediate credibility killer)
- Brand visibility that breaks entertainment immersion
- Gaming entries without genuine gaming culture understanding
- Theatrical/streaming entries without awareness of industry economics and release strategy language
- Ignoring the specific entertainment vertical's culture (Gaming vs Live vs Theatrical are very different juries)

### Language That Resonates
Audience engagement, cultural moment, franchise universe, release strategy, entertainment value, industry benchmark, award-season consideration, streaming performance, opening weekend, day-one sales, engagement rates within specific platforms.

### Red Flag Language
Brand awareness, impressions, reach, target audience alignment, communications objective, media mix.

### Medal Culture
More accessible than Cannes — Clio shows operate with a broader medal distribution philosophy. Gold, Silver, Bronze available across all categories, plus Grand Clio for exceptional work.

## Clio Sports 2026 (NEW SHOW PROFILE)

### Overview
- Dedicated to sports marketing across three advertiser types: Sponsor/Brand, Sports Property/Team, Media/Tech
- 16+ mediums including the unique NIL (Name, Image, Likeness) category
- Entry opens: April 21, 2026
- Final deadline: September 4, 2026
- Ceremony: timing aligned with sports marketing conference calendar

### Entry Fees (USD, 2026)
| Category | Fee |
|---|---|
| Gameday / Spot | $300 |
| Standard categories | $500–$650 |
| Integrated Campaign | $875 |

### What Juries Look For
Sports culture authenticity. Judges are sports industry insiders who will immediately identify if an entry doesn't understand the sport, the athlete, or the fan. The athletic/fan insight must lead, not the brand positioning. Results should include fan engagement metrics, cultural impact in sports media, and business outcomes relevant to the sports partnership.

### Jury Kill Factors
- Leading with brand positioning instead of the sports/fan insight
- Entries that could have been for any brand in any category — no sports specificity
- NIL category entries that treat athletes as media channels rather than creative collaborators
- Ignoring the specific sport's culture and fan community language
- Activation entries without evidence of genuine fan/athlete engagement

### Language That Resonates
Fan community, athlete authenticity, game-day moment, cultural alignment with sport, partnership equity, media value of sports properties, NIL authenticity, in-stadium impact, digital fan engagement.

### Red Flag Language
Brand building, awareness, generic "sports fan" audience without specificity, corporate partnership announcement, league sponsorship without activation insight.

### Medal Culture
Clio Sports medals are meaningful in the sports marketing industry. Gold at Clio Sports is the Sports Marketer of the Year award equivalent for campaign work.

## Clio Creators 2026 (BRAND NEW SHOW — LAUNCHED 2026)

### Overview
- First year of operation — launched 2026
- Brand partnership: YouTube and Influential (creator economy platforms)
- Two tracks: Brand Track (brand-funded creator content) and Creator Track (creator-led work)
- Designed specifically for creator economy marketing

### Entry Fees (USD, 2026)
| Track | Fee |
|---|---|
| Creator Track | $100 |
| Brand Track | $500 |
| Student | Free |

### Key Deadlines
- First deadline: April 24, 2026
- Final deadline: July 31, 2026

### What Juries Look For (Year One)
As a first-year show, Clio Creators is establishing its benchmarks with this inaugural cycle. Judges are expected to reward work where the creator's authentic voice is genuinely intact, the brand partnership feels natural rather than forced, and the content serves the creator's audience first. Creator community credibility is the primary evaluator.

### Critical 2026 Insight — Brand Visibility vs Creator Authenticity
The primary tension in creator marketing awards: entries where the brand's hand is visible over the creator's authentic voice are PENALISED at emerging shows in this space (and at Cannes Lions as of 2025). The winning entry is one where brand integration feels organic to the creator's established content style.

### Jury Kill Factors
- Brand messaging that overrides creator voice or tone
- Content that looks like a produced ad placed in a creator format
- Creator partnerships that lack genuine product-audience alignment
- Entries without evidence of authentic creator community engagement
- Re-purposed brand TV spots formatted as creator content

### Language That Resonates
Creator voice, authentic integration, community-first, platform-native format, creator equity, organic engagement, audience trust, fan relationship, long-term creator partnership.

### Red Flag Language
Brand ambassador, spokesperson, product placement (traditional sense), reach metrics without engagement depth, follower count as primary success metric.

## Effie Awards 2026 (US)

### Overview
- The global standard for marketing effectiveness
- Key change 2026: Global Multi-Region Effie category retired January 2026 — global campaigns now entered via each regional Effie separately
- ~40 industry categories plus specialty categories (Pro Bono, Small Budget, Sustained Success, etc.)
- US entry fees: $995–$4,160 depending on category tier

### Scoring Framework (4 Pillars — Equal Weight Groups)
| Pillar | Weight |
|---|---|
| Results | 30% |
| Challenge, Context & Objectives | 23.3% |
| Insights & Strategy | 23.3% |
| Bringing the Strategy to Life | 23.3% |

### What Juries Look For
Airtight logic chain from business challenge to strategic insight to creative execution to attributable results. Juries include CMOs and CFOs who have had to defend ROI to boards — they read the footnotes, check the methodology, and look for confounding factors. Every claim must be supported. Results must be contextualised within the market environment.

### Round One Killer — #1 Reason Entries Fail at Effie
**Retrofitted objectives.** Entries where the objectives written in the paper don't match what the brand was actually trying to achieve at campaign launch, or where objectives appear to have been reverse-engineered to match the results. Effie juries are experienced enough to recognise when objectives seem suspiciously aligned with outcomes. Objectives must be specific, pre-campaign, and realistic. Vague objectives ("increase brand awareness") with vague results ("brand awareness increased") fail immediately.

### Jury Kill Factors (2026)
- Retrofitted/reverse-engineered objectives (most common and most fatal)
- Results without methodology explanation
- Missing market baseline — results mean nothing without context
- Claiming effectiveness without establishing attribution logic
- "Increased brand awareness" as primary result without measurement detail
- Case entries that read as creative case studies rather than business effectiveness papers
- Global Multi-Region Effie entries — this category no longer exists in 2026

### Language That Resonates
Business challenge, market context, attribution methodology, baseline measurement, penetration, share of wallet, share of voice, revenue contribution, category growth rate, consumer panel data, sales uplift, third-party validation.

### Red Flag Language
Impressions, reach, awareness (unless specifically measured), engagement, content views, social mentions (as primary effectiveness proof), brand love.

### Medal Culture
Effie Golds are extremely hard to win. The show operates with a rigorous evidence threshold. Finalists are a meaningful achievement — making the Effie shortlist demonstrates a level of effectiveness rigour that most campaigns cannot match.

## Cross-Show Intelligence Notes (2026)

### AI Disclosure (All Shows)
Cannes Lions mandates AI disclosure from 2024. D&AD and other major shows are implementing similar policies in 2026. Best practice: disclose AI use in the technical/production section of entries, not in the body copy. Non-disclosure where AI played a material role is treated as a violation.

### Effectiveness Evidence Raising the Bar
Across all shows, jury conversations in 2025-26 show a consistent pattern: attribution rigour is being demanded at higher levels. Correlation is no longer accepted as causation. Creative shows (Cannes, One Show) increasingly require business results. Effectiveness shows (Effie) increasingly require the creative work to be genuinely brave, not just competent.

### International Jury Context
For all shows with international juries: always explain local cultural context. Do not assume jury members from other markets understand why a specific local platform, cultural moment, or consumer behaviour is significant. Translate not just language but significance.
